Why declination changes

The apparent solar path is tilted 23.44° to Earth’s equator. Declination is negative southward and positive northward, reaching zero at both equinoxes. At the Northern summer solstice, +23.44° declination places the Sun directly overhead at the Tropic of Cancer: 100% ideal efficiency.

What the polar lines show

At a solstice, the pole tilted toward the Sun remains illuminated while the opposite polar region remains dark. The Arctic and Antarctic Circle labels update live.

About the geocentric view

This is a viewpoint model, not a claim that the Sun physically orbits Earth. Keeping Earth centered makes the seasonal lighting geometry easier to compare.
